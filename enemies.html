ENEMIES

Overworld

Goblin

Goblins were a race of small and numerous goblinoids common throughout Toril, often living in underground caverns near the surface known as lairs. The race was often, though not always, dominated by other goblinoids, most commonly hobgoblins.

Bandit

Bandits rove in gangs and are sometimes led by thugs, veterans, or spellcasters. Not all bandits are evil. Oppression, drought, disease, or famine can often drive otherwise honest folk to a life of banditry.

Black Bear

The black bear is a forest-dwelling omnivore that usually is not dangerous unless an interloper threatens its cubs or food supply.

Giant Spider

To snare its prey, a giant Spider spins elaborate webs or shoots sticky strands of webbing from its abdomen. Giant spiders are most commonly found Underground, making their lairs on ceilings or in dark, web-filled crevices.

Gnoll

Gnolls are a humanoid race that most closely resembled human-hyena hybrids within Faerûn. They were carnivorous humanoids, known for their savage culture and warlike ways.

Gnoll Pack Lord

Gnolls Pack Lords are a humanoid race that most closely resembled human-hyena hybrids within Faerûn. They are carnivorous humanoids, and lead packs of lesser gnolls

Harpy

A harpy had the upper body of a female humanoid and the lower body of an reptilian creature, with scaly legs, clawed feet, and clawed hands with knotty fingers. Harpies had coal-black eyes in faces like cruel old women, with hair that was filthy, tangled, and crusted with old dry blood.

Ankheg

The ankheg is a huge insectoid monster with many slender limbs and large antennae. It burrows underground and hunted from below with poison.

Manticore

A manticore was a large, magical beast with the body of a lion, dragon-like wings, and a somewhat humanoid head. Its long tail had a cluster of deadly spikes, which the creature could launch at its foes as weapons. It has incredibly tough claws and fangs.

Shoalar Quanderil

Shoalar is a pirate captain that always appears jovial, but with a biting sense of humor and tending to laugh at people rather than with them.

Haunted Tombs

Goblin

Goblins were a race of small and numerous goblinoids common throughout Toril, often living in underground caverns near the surface known as lairs. The race was often, though not always, dominated by other goblinoids, most commonly hobgoblins.

Half Ogre

Half-ogres had a height somewhere between that of a human and an ogre and taller than a half-orc often between seven and eight feet (210 and 240 centimeters).

Spectre

A specter appeared as a humanoid, with a mostly transparent and faintly luminous form. They looked much as they did in life, though the injuries that caused their violent deaths were visible on many specimen and could be recognized by someone who knew what the person looked like

Flying Sword

A magically animated flying sword dances through the air, fighting like a warrior that can’t be injured. While the sword remains motionless and isn't flying, it is indistinguishable from a normal sword.

Flying Axe

A magically animated flying axe dances through the air, fighting like a warrior that can’t be injured. While the sword remains motionless and isn't flying, it is indistinguishable from a normal axe.

Lance Rock

Zombie

A zombie was an undead creature usually created by the re-animation of a corpse. Unlike animated skeletons, zombies still retained some flesh on their bodies, and gave off a horrid, rank smell. They were almost mindless, but could be given simple commands, such as "kill anyone who opens the treasure chest."

Skeleton

Skeletons were undead animated corpses similar to zombies, but completely devoid of flesh and did not feed on the living. They could be made from virtually any solid creature, and as such their size and power varied widely.

Skeleton Mage

Skeletal mages are minor spellcasters (typically 5th-level or less) who have retained both their intelligence and their spellcasting abilities.

Crawling Claw

Crawling claws were the amputated and then reanimated hands of humanoids. They were employed by dark wizards and warlocks to provide an extra set of hands or perform guard duty.

Ghoul

A ghoul appeared as an emaciated, roughly humanoid creature with an almost-hairless mottled, decaying hide stretched tight over its bones. It had the sharp teeth of a carnivore and sunken eyes that burned as if they were hot coals.

Orieth, Lord of Lance Rock

Oreioth had always been cruel, but after his self-exile to Red Larch with only the company of his undead servitors, he became a true megalomaniac. He barely recognized his own name and dreamed of founding a kingdom of undead minions with himself on the throne.[2] He considered himself the "Lord of Lance Rock" and hoped to protect his "kingdom" from invaders.

Tomb of the Moving Stones

Giant Rate

Giant rats, also known as dire rats, were dangerous creatures.[2] They were much larger than the common rat and were found in dungeons and city sewers.

Bringer of Woe

Medium armored cloaked figures that followed the commands of Larrakh to protect him and manipulate the people of Red Larch.

Dust Mephit

A dust mephit was about four feet (120 centimeters) tall and weighed a couple pounds (about one kilogram).[1] They had rough gray skin, which seemed to produce dust and grit constantly as they moved.[3] Like all mephits, dust mephits were impish in appearance and had wispy wings.

Animated Statue

An animated statue is a statue that was crafted with magic to follow the command of its creators.

Larrakh

The man manupulating the Believers. The priest hoped to redeem himself in the eyes of his cultist masters by conquering Red Larch. His plot was to transform the Believers into a nefarious organization.

Feathergale Spire

Giant Vulture

Giant vultures were a type of malevolent and unusually intelligent bird. Knight in the Feathergale Society ride them into battle.

Griffon

A griffon was an intelligent beast with the body of a lion and the wings, forelegs, and head of an eagle. These creatures were often used as heraldry symbols and mounts by skilled knights

Feathergale Initiate

Low ranking initiates in the Feathergale Society that train to one day become full fledged knights.

Feathergale Knight

Knights in the Feathergale Society that mount Giant vultures and griffons into battle. They wear wing suits so they can parachute to the ground.

Thurl Merroska

Thurl was once a griffon rider in Waterdeep until a great storm nearly killed him. After that experience, he gathered around him a group of like-minded wealthy folk of Waterdeep and founded the Feathergale Knights.

Feathergale Priest

Priests like figures that were seen at feathergale spire. The group did not see them in battle.

Sacred Stone Monastery

Sacred Stone Monk

Monks that occupy the Sacred Stone Monastery. They have given up all of their worldly possessions and wear robes with gargoyle masks. The monks train and do chores all day on a strict schedule.

Gargoyle

These malevolent creatures of elemental earth resemble grotesque, fiendish statues. A gargoyle lurks among masonry and ruins, delighting in the terror it creates when it breaks from its suspended pose.

Duregar

Duergar also known as gray dwarves,[9] were a subterranean subrace of dwarves.[1][2] They carved out an existence in the Underdark, often near volcanoes.

Minotaur

Minotaurs were evil giant muscular humanoids with fur-covered bodies and bull-like heads. It was once believed that all minotaurs were male; actually cursed humans that had been altered by the gods or a demon lord to become a minotaur.

Black Earth Guard

Heavily armored individuals that wear spiked stone armor and wield morningstar's.

Black Earth Priest

A priest-like figure that resides in the Sacred Stone Monastery. He is dressed exactly the same as Larrahk.

Hellenrae

A blind monk that is very skilled in hand-to-hand combat. Hellenrae is feared by everyone so they try to avoid contact with others. She greatly despised weakness and will challenge any new foe to test their strength.
